Our purpose

InHarmony's purpose is to provide teens a safe therapy space, whether it's digital or face-to-face. When teens reach adolescence, they could have a very difficult time with it. It's important for them to have a place to rely on, which could welcome them and let them know that they feel accepted and understood. Sometimes they could be going through problems that may affect their mental health. There are many aspects that could contribute such as: academic issues, bullying from school communities, high expectations from others, not being acknowledge as a person, and many more. These could often lead to teens keeping these feelings to themselves, and being afraid to communicate or express their emotions with other people. Which could then lead to social anxiety, depression, and other mental health related problems. That is why we're here. To serve, provide, and guide teens to a better environment and way of thinking. Through helping them express their feelings, and to have more balance and control.

Affordable and Negotiable services

We understand that therapy is a difficult thing to acquire in order to find guidance and support with mental health issues. One of the reasons why people are hesitant or afraid to get help from therapy is due to its high costs, while some can't afford it due to time limitations or budget constraints. Our proposal is to provide a sliding-scale or a negotiable payment option that can be balanced with the amount you can pay and the amount of time you can receive to make everything fair, there is no fixed cost because your health matters more than money.

Digital Guides

We know that understanding mental health can feel overwhelming without the right resources. Many people don't have access to consistent guidance or tools to support their growth and self-awareness. Our proposal is to provide easy-to-use digital guides that can be accessed anytime, anywhere. These guides will offer step-by-step strategies, coping techniques, and educational materials to help you navigate your mental health journey at your own pace.

Schedule Planning

Managing therapy sessions or self-care routines can be stressful, especially with busy schedules or unpredictable commitments. Some people struggle to maintain consistency because of work, school, or family responsibilities. Our solution is to provide flexible schedule planning, allowing sessions and activities to fit your lifestyle. This ensures that support is reliable and realistic, helping you prioritize your mental health without added pressure.

Psychological Tests

Understanding your mental health often requires clarity about your thoughts, emotions, and behaviors, which can be confusing to navigate alone. Many hesitate to explore this because traditional assessments can feel intimidating or inaccessible. We aim to provide clear and supportive psychological tests that are easy to complete and interpret. These tests will help identify areas of concern, track progress, and guide you toward the most effective strategies for your mental wellbeing.

Anxiety

Teen anxiety at school is triggered by academic pressure (grades, tests, workload), social challenges (bullying, peer rejection, performance fear), major life changes (new school, family stress, trauma), body image issues, lack of sleep, and underlying mental health conditions, all leading to intense worry about judgment, failure, or not fitting in.

Depression

Puberty triggers depression in teens through a mix of rapid hormonal shifts affecting mood, brain development (especially emotional regulation), and intensified social/academic pressures, leading to body image issues, peer conflict, identity struggles, family stress (like divorce or conflict), trauma (bullying, abuse), and pre-existing genetic vulnerabilities, all interacting to increase risk.

Socialising

Teens struggle with school socializing due to low self-esteem, social anxiety, fear of judgment, bullying, and difficulty reading social cues, leading to withdrawal, conflicts, or excessive people-pleasing under peer pressure. Mental health issues like depression, struggles with online interactions, and poor listening or understanding sarcasm also hinder connections, making everyday school life feel overwhelming and isolating.

Confidence

Teens struggle with school confidence due to academic pressure, social comparison (especially on social media), bullying, body image changes, family issues, mental health struggles like anxiety, and negative self-talk, leading to fear of failure, perfectionism, and avoiding challenges, impacting their focus and performance.
